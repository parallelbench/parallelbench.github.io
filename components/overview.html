Core Contributions

Why ParallelBench?

Parallel decoding is fast, but not free. Under conditional independence assumption, parallel decoding ignore dependencies, resulting in a drop in quality when tokens depend on each other. We provide information-theoretic analysis and quantitatively evaluate this trade-off using synthetic list operation tasks.

Left: Parallel decoding fails to capture token dependencies, risking incorrect combinations of individually likely tokens(e.g., "New" and "City"). Middle: Quantitative analysis of parallel decoding performance on list operations. Right: Based on our findings, we develop a realistic benchmark to evaluate the speed-quality trade-off of parallel decoding methods.

Information-Theoretic Analysis

Proves that parallel decoding has fundamental error bounds when tokens depend on each other, showing even perfect models struggle as we increase parallelism on tasks requiring strong token coordination.

Quantitative Case Studies

Analytically tractable synthetic list operations (Copy, Replace, Shuffle) with closed-form accuracy formulas demonstrate fundamental limitations: specific tasks show inevitable quality degradation under parallel decoding.

Realistic Benchmark Tasks

17 tasks across Waiting Line, Text Writing, and Puzzles—all trivial for humans and AR LLMs—reveal severe quality degradation in dLLMs under parallel decoding in real-world scenarios.
